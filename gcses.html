Where do I require poetry?
  • You will need to use your knowledge of the poems from the conflict cluster in order to pass your English Literature GCSE.
  • This requires you to memorise all required poems as well as their meanings and all literary devices employed.
  • However, also keep in mind that using the poetry syllabus is NOT the only part of the GCSE. You'll also need to be able to appreciate and evaluate both classic and modern prose. The qualification is also 100% exam-based.

What is the exam like?
  • Your English Language and English Literature exams should each last up to 2 hours at the most(subject to change). Within these exams, there are multiple questions about grammar, comprehension and texts studied and/or memorised.
  • From the Year 9 options booklet, here are the requirements from each exam:
  • LANGUAGE- GCSE English Language should enable students to: read a wide range of texts, fluently and with good understanding ; read critically, and use knowledge gained from wide reading to inform and improve their own writing; write effectively and coherently using Standard English appropriately ; use grammar correctly, punctuate and spell accurately; acquire and apply a wide vocabulary, alongside a knowledge and understanding of grammatical terminology and linguistic conventions for reading, writing and spoken language.
  • LITERATURE- GCSE English Literature should enable students to: read a wide range of classic literature fluently and with good understanding, and make connections across their reading; read in depth, critically and evaluatively, so that they are able to discuss and explain their understanding and ideas; develop the habit of reading widely and often; appreciate the depth and power of the English literary heritage; write accurately, effectively and analytically about their reading, using Standard English; acquire and use a wide vocabulary, including the grammatical terminology and other literary and linguistic terms they need to criticise and analyse what they read.
